The Questions Every Interior Design Client Questionnaire Should Ask

  • What colours are you naturally drawn to?
  • Which spaces make you feel most at ease?
  • What existing pieces would you like to keep?
  • How do you want your home to feel?

These questions are familiar to every interior design questionnaire, but answers are often vague, inconsistent, and open to interpretation. IMPRINT replaces written guesswork with instant visual clarity.

Frequently Asked

Your questions, answered.

Play Demo

What exactly do I get?

A window into how your client thinks. Before the meeting where they say "I'll know it when I see it."

Instead of walking in armed with a questionnaire full of vague adjectives, "cosy," "modern," "something with character," you walk in with their aesthetic DNA already mapped. Your client swipes through your curated images for seven minutes. You receive their dominant styles, colour instincts, the aesthetics they instinctively reject, and the contradictions they couldn't articulate if you interviewed them for two hours.

The brief almost writes itself. You look sharper, faster, more intuitive. And the entire experience is wrapped in your studio's branding, so to your client, this isn't software they downloaded. It's your methodology.

What if my client can't describe what they want?

That's the problem IMPRINT™ was built for.

Your clients know exactly what they want. They just can't tell you. Ask them to describe their style and you get words that mean different things to different people. Ask them to build a moodboard and they assemble a collage of contradictions. Ask them to fill a form and they freeze.

IMPRINT™ removes language from the equation entirely. They swipe. Their instincts reveal themselves, before they overthink, before they self-edit, before they Google "what is Japandi." You capture taste in its raw form. Then you translate it for them.

What if I show work and they say "that's not quite right"?

That moment costs you. A revision spiral. A strained relationship. A portfolio piece you can't photograph. Sometimes a client you never see again.

IMPRINT™ prevents it before it happens. Because knowing what your client loves is only half the intelligence. Knowing what they reject, instinctively, immediately, without needing to explain why, is what protects your work.

Every "no" they swipe becomes a signal. The styles, tones, and aesthetics that would have cost you a revision are flagged before you open your sketchbook. You don't just show the right thing. You know exactly what to avoid.

What if my clients are a couple who disagree?

You know that meeting. The one where partners contradict each other, where "compromise" means nobody gets what they want, where you become an awkward referee instead of a designer.

IMPRINT™ resolves the conflict before you walk in. Both partners swipe independently. The report shows where they align, where they pull apart, and what shared design language can hold both. You arrive with data, not diplomacy. You lead the conversation instead of mediating it.

The project starts on solid ground. And you never have to watch two people argue about throw pillows in your studio again.

What if I keep making wrong turns that cost me revisions?

You will. Until you have rejection data.

Most design tools tell you what your client liked. IMPRINT™ tells you what they disliked. That's the intelligence that saves your profit margin.

Every instinctive rejection is logged. The aesthetic directions that would have led to "can we try something else?" are visible before you commit time to them. You stop presenting options that miss the mark. Your first presentation lands closer to final. Your revision count drops. Your hourly rate stops getting diluted by unpaid do-overs.

This isn't about being more creative. It's about being more precise with the creativity you already have.

How does this make my studio stronger over time?

Every client who swipes adds to your proprietary intelligence.

The analytics dashboard tracks dominant styles across your client base, which images perform, where tastes are shifting. Six months in, you don't just know your current client better, you know your market better. You see trends before they hit Pinterest. You brief suppliers with data, not hunches. You build an advantage that quietly compounds while other studios are still guessing.

The studios that started early? They now have a data asset their competitors can't see coming. The gap between knowing and guessing widens every month.

The question isn't whether this becomes standard. It's whether your studio is the one others are trying to catch up to.
